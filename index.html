​Play On!

​LACHS makes games for a modern age. With our world increasingly making use of modern technology, LACHS embraces the notion that today's children will learn with computers. To ensure no child gets left behind, we bring our games to the internet, accessible across countless platforms. With cheaper technology solutions on the rise, this number can only grow. We hope kids everywhere have fun learning with LACHS!

Version control is a necessity to all programmers - it's a vital part of their education, just like those basic things children learn in primary schools. GitHub knows just how important it is to teach a good workflow and make sure everyone's on the same page, so we have partnered with them to employ a new line of ambassadors for our games!

Expect to see LACHS at a school near you very soon as we demonstrate our games to youngsters across the UK, provide fun tutorials, and show them that education can be fun with the magic of modern technology. GitHub's mentors are also very excited to work with us, and have released their own official statement on the matter, from the mouth of their very own little Octocat, Mona Lisa.

"I can't wait to show you all what LACHS have in store!"

"Learning can be tough, but this website makes it super fun," the Octocat has said as the mascot of her company in announcing our partnership. "I can't wait to show you all what LACHS have in store!"

GitHub are often present at hackathons across the UK and US, so they're much more used to working with university students. We can assure you, however, that they've all been fully trained to engage the interest of primary school children in our official LACHS-approved tutorial programme.

Perhaps LACHS will visit your child's school very soon? Watch this space for an official list of schools we'll be visiting.

LACHS releases its first game!

LACHS is proud to release our first game - a sliding block puzzle! Slide each piece into place to match the given image. It's random each time, so have fun trying to create not one, not two, but a possible 362,880 different pictures! And better yet, if you're struggling, just hit the reshuffle button for a new puzzle and a new starting point.

Read on for the technicalities...and if you don't know what that word means, perhaps you've got a little more learning to do. Either way, LACHS looks forward to helping as many children on their learning journeys as possible!

"It was tough, but we've finally done it."

Haswell worked for a straight day to put this game out. It is the culmination of Javascript experience he has picked up over the past several weeks, a language he chose for its extensive compatibility with all kinds of inputs and browsers. The game is fully playable on touchscreens and PCs alike, allowing it to reach children everywhere, no matter their device.

We're all very excited to have put this game out for everyone. As Brookes-Lachs proclaimed after a much-needed cup of tea, "It was tough, but we've finally done it." Enjoy the game, and await the next ones!

​We Recommend...

Club Penguin

In March 2005, the founders of Club Penguin set out to create an ad-free, virtual world where children could play games, have fun, and interact.

As internet specialists and parents, they wanted Club Penguin to be a place they'd feel comfortable letting their own children and grandchildren visit.

After months of consultation, research and testing, Club Penguin opened to the public in October 2005. Thanks to great reviews from players and parents, word quickly spread. In March 2006, Club Penguin launched on Miniclip, the world's largest online game site, and rapidly became its number one game.

In August 2007, Club Penguin joined the Walt Disney Company in a partnership designed to provide access to unprecedented resources and unique development opportunities.

Less than a year later, in April 2008, Club Penguin opened its first international office in Brighton, England, in order to provide localised player support and moderation.

Club Penguin is privileged to be part of the Walt Disney Company, which shares its unwavering commitment to creating a fun and safe online entertainment experience for children and families.

At Club Penguin we believe everyone deserves a place to live, good health, an education and hope for a bright future. We're passionate about community engagement and we work hard to foster a culture of giving, both in the virtual world of Club Penguin and within our company.

Club Penguin is headquartered in the beautiful city of Kelowna, British Columbia, Canada and maintains offices and staff in São Paulo, Brazil; and Buenos Aires, Argentina.

Neopets

Neopets® began way back in 1997 in a dingy little computer room. The site was launched on 15th November 1999. Neopets has been available in many languages since 2004 -- including Spanish, French, Portuguese, Simplified Chinese, Traditional Chinese, German and Dutch. Our aim is to keep adding new and exciting games, puzzles and activities daily and hopefully keep you all entertained!

Moshi Monsters

Moshi Monsters is an online game for kids, where they can adopt and take care of a friendly monster. It’s free to play, but we also offer a paid Moshi Membership that gives players access to a host of exclusive extra features.

Within the game there are many fun, educational activities for kids to enjoy, such as:

  • Playing games and solving puzzles to earn virtual rewards, called "Rox"
  • Growing and collecting pet ‘Moshlings’ in their garden
  • Socialising with friends and family
  • Reading the latest Moshi news and entering competitions on The Daily Growl blog

In fact, many parents (and grandparents) have so much fun helping to look after their children's monster that they adopt their own Moshi Monster. In addition, we sell cool Moshi Monsters merchandise that make great presents for the Monster Owners in your family. Visit the Official Moshi Monsters Online Store for the latest and greatest.

Accessibility

​Here at LACHS, we wish to ensure that this site is usable by any and all. As such, wherever possible, this site has been coded in a way that allows your browser to change certain features. In particular, those of you with sight impairments are able to use features such as high contrast mode and narration. If you do not have a pleasant user experience, please contact us.
